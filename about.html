Our story

RouteFlow London turns complex transport data into joyful guidance.

What began as a weekend project to track rare bus workings has grown into an inclusive studio for planners, schools, and families. We blend real-time feeds with narrative design so everyone can understand London’s network.

Why we exist

Transport knowledge should feel calm, colourful, and accessible.

RouteFlow London is built on three promises that guide every release.

Clarity first

We translate TfL feeds into friendly summaries, legible typography, and motion that supports rather than distracts.

Accessibility always

Every screen supports dark mode, high contrast, dyslexia-friendly fonts, and keyboard navigation by default.

Community powered

Fleet notes, withdrawn stock, and enthusiast insights are curated with credit to the people who share them.

How we build

Small teams, rapid experiments, and constant listening.

1

Prototype with purpose

We release features in weekly sprints, validating them with a community group of planners, drivers, and enthusiasts.

2

Audit accessibility every sprint

Each release is checked against WCAG guidelines and includes documented keyboard flows and motion alternatives.

3

Share openly

We publish changelogs, maintain a public roadmap, and invite Discord discussions so improvements stay collaborative.
